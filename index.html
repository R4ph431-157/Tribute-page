Dr Muhammad Yunus

The man who revolutionized poverty alleviation through microcredit

Here's a timeline of Dr Yunus’ Life:

  • 1940 - Born in Chittagong, Bangladesh, into a middle-class family.
  • 1965 - Earns a Fulbright scholarship and completes his Ph.D. in Economics at Vanderbilt University in the United States.
  • 1971 - Returns to Bangladesh during its independence war and witnesses extreme poverty and famine, which deeply impacts him.
  • 1974 - Inspired by the struggles of impoverished villagers in Jobra, he lends $27 from his pocket to 42 women to help them escape loan sharks, proving that small loans can change lives.
  • 1976 - Founds the Grameen Bank Project, introducing the concept of microcredit—small, collateral-free loans for the poor, especially women.
  • 1983 - Grameen Bank becomes an official bank, empowering millions of people through financial inclusion. Its innovative model focuses on trust rather than traditional banking systems.
  • 1997 - Launches the Microcredit Summit Campaign, uniting global leaders to provide microfinance solutions for poverty alleviation worldwide.
  • 2006 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for his efforts to create economic opportunities for the poor. He is recognized for showing that even the poorest of the poor can contribute to society if given access to opportunity.
  • 2008 - Creates the Grameen Foundation, expanding microfinance to include technology and healthcare solutions for underserved communities globally.
  • 2010 - Founds the Yunus Centre to promote his vision of social business, businesses aimed at solving social issues rather than maximizing profits.
  • 2011 - Steps down from Grameen Bank amidst political controversy but continues his work advocating for global poverty reduction, social business, and youth empowerment.
  • 2015 - Calls for using entrepreneurship to solve global challenges like poverty, unemployment, and climate change, emphasizing the power of “social business”.
  • 2023 - Passes away at the age of 83, leaving behind a legacy of empowerment, innovation, and hope for millions of people worldwide.
"Yunus’ life and vision have proven that small actions can spark revolutionary change. By empowering the poor, he not only transformed individual lives but also reshaped global economic systems with compassion and ingenuity."
